The multiparametric prostate MRI was performed to monitor the known prostate cancer. TECHNIQUE: Multiplanar, multisequence MRI of the abdomen and pelvis was performed, including diffusion, T2, and dynamic contrast-enhanced (DCE) imaging of the prostate. The post-contrast images were obtained following the uneventful intravenous administration of 8 mL of gadobutrol (Gadavist) gadolinium-containing contrast. COMPARISON: ( )FINDINGS: The prostate gland is mildly enlarged measuring 4.2 x 4.8 x 4.7 cm (volume 42 mL). There are no suspicious lesions or abnormal signal intensity in the peripheral zone of the prostate gland. However, a small focal area of low T2 signal intensity is seen in the right transition zone measuring approximately 6 mm in diameter, which has increased in size compared to the prior study. The area shows restricted diffusion on the ADC map, with a value of 912 x 10^-3 mm^2/s. In addition, there is a focal area of T2 hypointensity in the left base peripheral zone, measuring approximately 7 mm in diameter, which has remained stable. The ADC value for this area is 1235 x 10^-3 mm^2/s. The dynamic contrast-enhanced (DCE) characteristics show diffusely enhancing area with plateau-type kinetics in the right transition zone, with a PI-RADS v2 score of 4. On the other hand, the left base peripheral zone has mildly enhancing area with gradual wash-in and washout kinetics, with a PI-RADS v2 score of 3. IMPRESSION: Based on the above findings, there is no evidence of suspicious lesions or significant changes in the peripheral zone of the prostate gland compared to the prior study. However, the small focal area of low T2 signal intensity in the right transition zone has increased in size compared to the prior study, with a PI-RADS v2 score of 4. This suggests a possible progression of the previously diagnosed prostate cancer in the right transition zone, requiring further evaluation and monitoring. The patient will be advised for additional biopsy and follow-up by their urologist.

COMPARISON: There are no previous scans for comparison. FINDINGS: The prostate is found to be enlarged, measuring 4.4 x 5.3 x 4.6 cm, with a volume of 56 mL. Three lesions have been identified. Lesion L1 is located in the left anterior transition zone at the mid gland, not crossing the midline, and measures 10 x 9 mm. The lesion appears as a focal heterogeneously, moderately T2 hypointense lesion with circumscribed margins. There is no definite extra-prostatic extension or invasive behavior observed. The neurovascular bundles are not involved. The lesion has a PI-RADS v1 T2 score of 2 and a PI-RADS v2 T2 score of 3. The DWI/ADC values show an ADC region of interest value of 984 x10 -3 mm 2/s, with a PI-RADS v1 DWI/ADC score of 5 and a PI-RADS v2 DWI/ADC score of 3. The DCE results show a PI-RADS v1 DCE score of 3 and a PI-RADS v2 DCE score of positive. The overall PI-RADS v1 Lesion Summary Score is 4, and the PI-RADS v2 Lesion Summary Score is 3. Lesion R1 is located in the right anterior to posterolateral peripheral zone at the mid gland, not crossing the midline, and measures 11 x 7 mm. The lesion appears as a focal heterogeneously, mildly T2 hypointense lesion with circumscribed margins. There is no definite extra-prostatic extension or invasive behavior observed. The neurovascular bundles are not involved. The lesion has a PI-RADS v1 T2 score of 1 and a PI-RADS v2 T2 score of 2. The DWI/ADC values show an ADC region of interest value of 1067 x10 -3 mm 2/s, with a PI-RADS v1 DWI/ADC score of 5 and a PI-RADS v2 DWI/ADC score of 3. The DCE results show a PI-RADS v1 DCE score of 2 and a PI-RADS v2 DCE score of negative. The overall PI-RADS v1 Lesion Summary Score is 3, and the PI-RADS v2 Lesion Summary Score is 2. Lesion R2 is located in the right posterolateral peripheral zone at the base, not crossing the midline, and measures 8 x 5 mm. The lesion appears as an ovoid heterogeneously, moderately T2 hypointense lesion with partially circumscribed, partially indistinct margins. There is no definite extra-prostatic extension or invasive behavior observed. The neurovascular bundles are not involved. The lesion has a PI-RADS v1 T2 score of 3 and a PI-RADS v2 T2 score of 3. The DWI/ADC values show an ADC region of interest value of 1037 x10 -3 mm 2/s, with a PI-RADS v1 DWI/ADC score of 5 and a PI-RADS v2 DWI/ADC score of 4. The DCE results show a PI-RADS v1 DCE score of 2 and a PI-RADS v2 DCE LCA continue score of negative. The overall PI-RADS v1 Lesion Summary Score is 4, and the PI-RADS v2 Lesion Summary Score is 4. The peripheral zone of the prostate demonstrates uniform high signal intensity on T2 without additional abnormalities on diffusion or postcontrast imaging, suggesting it is likely benign. The central gland exhibits a well-encapsulated whorled appearance, consistent with benign prostatic hyperplasia, also indicating that it is likely benign. Examining the remaining abdomen and pelvis, the lung bases are clear, and no pleural or pericardial effusions are observed. The liver appears normal in morphology and signal intensity, with no focal liver lesions identified. Conventional hepatic arterial anatomy is present, and the hepatic, portal, and superior mesenteric veins are patent. No intra- or extrahepatic biliary dilatation is present, and the gallbladder is normal without gallstones. The pancreas, adrenal glands, and spleen are normal. Bilateral small renal cysts are noted, measuring up to 17 mm in the right kidney, with no solid mass or hydronephrosis observed. The bowel appears normal. The bladder is trabeculated, consistent with chronic bladder outlet obstruction and diverticulosis. The visualized aorta is normal in caliber, and the IVC is patent. No enlarged lymph nodes are detected in the abdomen or pelvis, and no free fluid is seen in the abdomen or pelvis. Multilevel degenerative changes of the spine are noted. Lesion R1 in the mid region of the central gland of the prostate is equivocal for prostate cancer, with a PI-RADSv1 category 3 and PI-RADSv2 category 2.
