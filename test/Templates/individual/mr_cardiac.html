Mild global hypokinesis of the left ventricle with an ejection fraction of 48%. Small pericardial effusion. RECOMMENDATIONS: Clinical correlation is advised to determine the relevance of these findings in relation to the patient's symptoms. Further evaluation and management of myocarditis should be discussed with the patient's healthcare provider, considering the patient's symptoms and physical examination findings. Free TextEXAMINATION: Cardiac MRI PATIENT HISTORY: A 65-year-old male with a history of hypertension and dyspnea on exertion. Evaluation for possible hypertrophic cardiomyopathy is requested. TECHNIQUE: 3 Tesla MRI scanner. The study includes cine imaging in standard long-axis and short-axis views, T2-weighted imaging, T2* imaging, late gadolinium enhancement (LGE) sequences, and volumetric quantification measurements. FINDINGS: The left ventricle demonstrates asymmetric septal hypertrophy with a maximal wall thickness of 18 mm in the basal anteroseptal segment. The left ventricular ejection fraction is preserved at approximately 55%. The right ventricle appears normal in size and function. Mild aortic regurgitation is observed, and the other valves appear normal. No significant pericardial effusion is identified. Late gadolinium enhancement reveals patchy mid-myocardial enhancement in the hypertrophied septal segments, suggestive of myocardial fibrosis. T2-weighted images show no signs of myocardial edema or inflammation. T2* imaging reveals a myocardial T2* value of 32 milliseconds, indicating no significant myocardial iron overload. The atria are normal in size, and no intracardiac thrombus is detected. Preserved left ventricular ejection fraction at 55%. Patchy mid-myocardial enhancement in the hypertrophied septal segments, suggestive of myocardial fibrosis. Mild aortic regurgitation and no significant pericardial effusion. No evidence of significant myocardial iron overload based on T2* value.
